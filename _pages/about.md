I'm Xuelin (Shellyn) Yang, born and raised in Wuhan, Hubei, China. I am currently an undergraduate at Haverford College pursuing a B.S. in Chemistry (ACS Certified) and a B.A. in Mathematics (Bryn Mawr College). My research spans computational materials science, solid-state chemistry, and biomedical imaging — from molecular dynamics simulations of peptide–nanoparticle interactions to DFT modeling of 2D MXene materials.

I am passionate about applying computational and experimental tools to understand complex chemical systems at the atomic scale.

## Research Interests

- Density functional theory (DFT) and *ab initio* methods for 2D materials
- Molecular dynamics simulations of biomolecule–nanomaterial interfaces
- High-throughput materials discovery

## Current Work

I am currently a thesis undergraduate researcher in the Daly Lab at Haverford College, where I use molecular dynamics simulations and metadynamics sampling to investigate how post-translational modifications (glycosylation, phosphorylation) affect peptide binding to silica nanoparticles.

Previously, I was a summer researcher at Vanderbilt University (NSF M-STAR Center) modeling tridentate ligand–MXene interactions via DFT, and at the University of Pennsylvania studying CT image reconstruction using photon-counting detectors.
